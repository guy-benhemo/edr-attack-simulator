# Guardz Visual Elements Guide

## Photography

- Use relatable personas for clients and prospects
- Ensure the Guardz product is visible in any screen or device shown
- Include diverse individuals to connect with a wider audience
- **Don't** use images that look like stock photos
- **Don't** use goofy humor
- **Don't** show outdated "futuristic" clichés

## Iconography

### Platform Icons
When referring to features from the platform, use the platform's feature icons.

### Small Size Icons
- Primary green (#4FE882) for emphasis
- Purple gradient for remaining elements
- Keep icons simple and clear — never use detailed icons at small sizes

### Large Size Icons
- More detailed and illustrative icon family
- Use when icons have enough space to show detail

### Icon Don'ts
- Don't add outlines to icons
- Don't add shadows to icons
- Don't use detailed icons in small sizes

## Background Elements

Use these patterns for visual texture and brand reinforcement:
- Square grid
- Guardz logo hinted (subtle watermark)
- Dotted grid
- Icons & symbols in low opacity

## Illustrations

Guardz uses illustrations to bring the brand identity to life and build emotional connection.

**Color coding:**
- **Guardianz** (brand characters/heroes) → Green color family
- **Guardz Clients** (users/MSPs) → Purple color family

## Effects

Two primary shadow/effect styles:
- **Primary Dark Shadow** — for dark-themed elements
- **Primary Purple Shadow** — for brand-colored elements

## Screenshots & Product Visuals

When showing the product in marketing or documentation:
- Genuinely capture the true look and feel of the product
- When highlighting a specific part, either:
  - Scale and crop that section of the screen, OR
  - Highlight it using green illustrated arrows
- Always use real, up-to-date product screenshots
